Switch

Aki delivered results fast, but was still losing ground to all the needs that were absolutely required immmediately, or even preferably for yesterday.

Monks under his rule were often working late or during weekends. As this showed no signs of slowing down, some began to call in sick.

Haru was more prudent, and his teams were and willing to take the blame for not accepting all the requirements, or delaying some. Better by far, he said, to produce only one perfect application than lose focus and disappoint customers.

He was soon accused of letting the monks becoming lazy.

— — —

Upon arriving to the Spider Clan, Aki immediately announced the monks would be rated on /quantity/: how many lines of code were produced, tickets completed, standalone applications produced.

Surely, this would please the Emperor and Province Governor.

But Haru scoffed at these rules: his was the way of /quality/: he immediately set up the guidelines, the meetings and documents that should be produced before working, like True Engineers should.
