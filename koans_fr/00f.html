Chorale

It’s a very little known fact that the Temple has indeed, a chorus of singing monks. Master Bawan never fails to show up during the practices, but never sings a note.

Master Kaimu noticed that and came one evening. After the lesson, he confronted: “It seems you never sing at the lessons, brother.”

“Indeed I am not,” smiled Bawan.

“But this poor soul sometimes wish to sleep and as your quarters’ neighbour, quite often his meditations are troubled with the most unpleasant shrieks someone might call singing,” reproached Kaimu.

“Indeed I am,” smiled Bawan.

“Explain,” growled Kaimu, “why you keep your lips shut where it is clearly the place and time to sing, and why you keep hurting my ears when it’s not appropriate.”

“These are my project management lessons,” explained Bawan. “I like to see how people work in unison, and how to fix disonance.”

Kaimu’s reply went sharp: “And your singing practice at night?”

“Whoever wishes to get better at anything must try new things. You must be willing to fail, and give it full effort. Avoiding being judged during that process helps a lot. And of course, this must be something you’re not good at already.”
