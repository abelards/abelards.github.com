Smart Watch

It was summer, the air was warm with a pleasing breeze, and Master Banzen asked his prentice the nun Satou to do a code kata, some kind of reporting he’d like to run occasionnally in the future.

She went in the Temple’s courtyard and ran the commands and scripts. She thought about the solution’s steps and validated it with her terminal. That gave her enough data to test the next versions, then she wrote the first bricks of her solution, then an ugly version, then made it more elegant, and was happy to see it was quite fast.

A few monks’ names rose up to her screen. Bawan passed and said, “well, these monks have well deserved a gift from me.”

= Qi’s Commentary

He who uses an ugly fix flogs himself four times: - by having to hack - by hacking - by living with the hacks’ consequences - by having to remove the hacks And these are, before the masters find out.

= Qi’s Poem

Using an ugly fix
makes you suffer four times

By the dangerous mix
requiring these lil’ crimes

By having to come up
with such a solution
And somehow giving up
hopes of resolution
In the time one has got
which is often short
To do like the abbot
didn’t get it but shot
Whatever code you fling
aware of your failings But code’s a moving thing
and you will be living With its consequences
